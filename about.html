About the Conference

The International Conference on Smart Control, Communication, and AI-Driven Technologies for Future Power and Energy Systems aims to bring together researchers, academicians, industry experts, and innovators to explore emerging advancements shaping the next generation of power and energy infrastructures

The conference provides a multidisciplinary platform to discuss intelligent control strategies, advanced communication technologies, artificial intelligence applications, smart grid innovations, renewable energy integration, power electronics, and cyberphysical systems that are transforming the energy sector. As the global energy landscape rapidly evolves, the need for smart, secure, efficient, and reliable power systems has become critical. This conference addresses these challenges by promoting the exchange of knowledge, cutting-edge research findings, technological trends, and industrial best practices.

About the Department

The Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE) at Anurag University enlightens the students with an academic benchmark that instils in them an electrifying and enriching educational framework. Accredited by the National Board of Accreditation (NBA), the EEE department assures a promising future for the students at Anurag University.

The EEE department was conceptualized in 2002 by introducing B. Tech program with current intake of 120. The department has Power Electronics and Electrical Drives (PEED) as PG specialization with an intake of 36 students. The department has been a hive of academic activity and instrumental in creating successful careers for many students. The Department has well equipped laboratories with sophisticated lab equipment. Consistent growth was witnessed in development and consequently the Department was accredited by NBA in 2008, 2017 and reaccredited by NBA in 2022.

The Department has strong industry interaction and signed MoU's with 30 core industries. It offers Internships and Placements in Core Industries through this Interaction with Industry thus bridging the gap between Industry and Academics.
